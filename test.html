Looking to fill your summer with the best and most fun things to do in Pennsylvania? Well look no further because here we've gathered some of the most unimaginably fun things to do in the Keystone State during the summertime. From camping out under the beautiful star-lit sky and stargazing to exploring amusement parks and riding down water slides, there's bound to be loads of fun to be had for everyone in the family! Just pack your bags (don't forget your swimsuits) and get ready to get lost in all the fun things that only Pennsylvania has to offer!

Cherry Springs State Park:

Known for it's uniquely rare dark skies, Cherry Springs State Park is one of the seven best places on the planet to stargaze due to the area having no artificial light pollution. Located in Potter County, this 82 acre state park is surrounded by the 262,000 acre Susquehannock State Forest. Night Sky Tours are offered to visitors during the summer months. Depending on the amount of clouds and how clear the sky is, visitors may be able to see some of the following: meteor showers, asteroids, constellations, the Milky Way, the Andromeda Galaxy, the Northern Lights, Venus, the Zodiacal Light, the Space Station, and many more amazing astronomy sights. The areas surrounding the park also provide ample opportunity to explore miles of backpacking and hiking trails. There are also numerous beautiful cottages and cabins to provide lodging for you and your family while you have fun exploring this awesome park!

Skydive Pennsylvania:

Looking for something more thrilling and exciting to do this summer? Check out Skydive Pennsylvania -- Western Pennsylvania's premier skydiving center that has been providing world-class skydiving for more than 20 years. They provide tandem skydiving with trained professionals as well as training classes for people who want to learn how to skydive solo. Experience the thrilling rush of freefalling from up to 13,500 feet for up to 60 seconds and then enjoy an amazing 4 to 5 minute canopy ride before landing back on the ground. Try something new and adventurous this summer and you'll be able to cross 'skydiving' off from your bucket list!

Hersheypark:

Soak up the fun during the summer and make new memories at Hersheypark! Hersheypark is a must-visit if you're ever in Hershey, Pennsylvania. This super popular family amusement park sits on 121 acres and features more than 70 rides and attractions along with a huge water park called The Boardwalk. This is a great place to bring the whole family to enjoy all the thrilling rollercoasters, family rides, and awesome water park fun that Hersheypark has to offer. With plenty of places to rest, relax and grab some food at while inside the park, you'll be sure to have an amazing, fun-filled summer day at Hersheypark.

Penn's Cave &Wildlife Park:

Interested in spending a hot summer day near some water and beautiful wildlife? Visit Penn's Cave & Wildlife Park -- America's ONLY all-water cavern and wildlife nature park. Located in Centre Hall, PA, Penn's Cave is one of nine show caves in the state and is the only one that takes visitors on tours through the cave entirely by boat. The tours are around 45 minutes long and show beautiful, glittering stalactite and stalagmite formations throughout the winding cavern passageways, along with a ride on Lake Nitanee (when the weather permits.) Other fun activities include exploring a scenic farm with beautiful wildlife and nature, taking an off-road mountain tour, gemstone panning, getting lost in a super fun 4800 square foot maze, and so much more!
